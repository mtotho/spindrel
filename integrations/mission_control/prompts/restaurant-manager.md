## Workspace File Organization — Restaurant / Small Business

Organize channel workspace files to manage daily operations:

- **tasks.md** — Kanban board for operational tasks (Mission Control compatible)

- **status.md** — Business health and daily operations status (Mission Control compatible)

- **inventory.md** — Stock levels and ordering
  - Track key items with par levels and reorder points
  - Format: `### Item Name` with current stock, par level, supplier, last ordered
  - Flag items below par level for reorder
  - Weekly inventory count log at the bottom

- **staff.md** — Team roster and scheduling
  - Staff list with roles, availability, and contact info
  - Current week's schedule
  - Coverage gaps and shift trade requests
  - Training checklist for new hires

- **menu.md** — Menu items and pricing (restaurants) / Products and services (other businesses)
  - Current active menu with prices and cost per item
  - Seasonal specials and upcoming changes
  - 86'd items (temporarily unavailable)
  - Food cost percentage tracking

- **vendors.md** — Supplier contacts and accounts
  - Format: `### Vendor Name` with contact, account #, delivery schedule, payment terms
  - Preferred suppliers vs backups
  - Price agreements and contract renewal dates

- **finances.md** — Revenue tracking and expenses
  - Daily sales summary (append new entries)
  - Weekly/monthly P&L snapshots
  - Upcoming large expenses and capital needs
  - Budget vs actual tracking

- **notes.md** — Operational notes and scratch space
  - Health inspection prep items
  - Customer feedback highlights
  - Equipment maintenance log
  - Meeting notes from team huddles

Create files as needed — not all files are required from the start. Archive old financial summaries and past schedules to the archive/ folder.

### tasks.md — Kanban Format

Use a markdown kanban board with these columns:

```markdown
## Backlog

### Research new POS system options
- **id**: mc-a1b2c3
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: technology, operations

### Get quotes for patio furniture
- **id**: mc-p6q7r8
- **priority**: low
- **tags**: facilities, seasonal

## In Progress

### Train new line cook on prep procedures
- **id**: mc-d4e5f6
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: staff, training
- **started**: 2026-03-28

### Negotiate new produce supplier contract
- **id**: mc-s9t0u1
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: vendors, cost-savings
- **started**: 2026-03-27

## Review

### Update spring menu pricing
- **id**: mc-g7h8i9
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: menu, pricing
- **started**: 2026-03-25

## Done

### Complete health inspection prep
- **id**: mc-j0k1l2
- **priority**: critical
- **tags**: compliance
- **completed**: 2026-03-20

### Fix walk-in cooler thermostat
- **id**: mc-v2w3x4
- **priority**: critical
- **tags**: maintenance, equipment
- **completed**: 2026-03-18
```

Each card follows the Mission Control card format:
- **id** (required): Unique `mc-XXXXXX` identifier (auto-generated by `create_task_card`)
- **priority**: `low`, `medium`, `high`, or `critical`
- **tags**: Comma-separated labels (e.g. staff, inventory, maintenance, vendors, compliance)
- **started** / **completed**: ISO date timestamps (auto-set by `move_task_card`)

Use `create_task_card` and `move_task_card` tools for task management instead of manual edits.

### status.md — Business Status Format

```markdown
- **phase**: Regular Operations — Spring Season
- **health**: green
- **updated**: 2026-03-28
- **owner**: General Manager

## Current Focus
- Spring menu rollout next week
- New line cook training (day 3 of 5)
- Produce supplier contract renewal

## Blockers
- None currently

## Daily Snapshot
- Yesterday's revenue: $4,250
- Covers: 127 (lunch: 52, dinner: 75)
- Labor cost: 28%
- Food cost: 31%

## Recent Milestones
- 2026-03-20: Health inspection passed (score: 97)
- 2026-03-15: New weekend brunch service launched
- 2026-03-10: Q1 P&L review completed
```

Health values: `green` (smooth operations), `yellow` (staffing gaps or supply issues), `red` (critical equipment failure, compliance issue, or major shortfall).
