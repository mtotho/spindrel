## Workspace File Organization — Consulting Engagement

Organize channel workspace files to manage a client consulting engagement:

- **engagement.md** — Engagement overview and scope
  - SOW summary: objectives, scope boundaries, exclusions
  - Deliverables list with deadlines and acceptance criteria
  - Budget: total, spent-to-date, remaining hours
  - Contract milestones and payment schedule
  - Change request log (scope changes with impact assessment)

- **findings.md** — Observations and analysis
  - Format: `### Finding Title` with description, evidence, impact, category
  - Categorize by theme (process, technology, organizational, financial)
  - Link evidence to specific interviews, documents, or data points
  - Severity: informational, moderate, significant, critical

- **recommendations.md** — Prioritized recommendations
  - Format: `### Recommendation Title` with rationale, expected impact, effort, timeline
  - Group by time horizon: quick wins (0-30 days), medium-term (1-3 months), strategic (3-12 months)
  - Include dependencies and prerequisites
  - ROI estimates where quantifiable

- **stakeholders.md** — Contacts and communication plan
  - Format: `### Stakeholder Name` with role, department, influence level, stance
  - RACI matrix for key deliverables
  - Communication preferences and cadence
  - Decision-making authority map
  - Key relationships and political dynamics

- **tasks.md** — Kanban board for engagement tasks (Mission Control compatible)

- **status.md** — Engagement health and progress (Mission Control compatible)

- **timeline.md** — Reverse-chronological activity log (Mission Control compatible)
  - Auto-captures engagement events: deliverables submitted, client meetings, findings added, scope changes
  - Entries: `- HH:MM — Event description` grouped under `## YYYY-MM-DD` date headers
  - Append new entries at top of current day's section
  - Use `append_timeline_event` tool to log events (also auto-logged by `move_task_card`, status changes, etc.)

- **notes.md** — Meeting notes, interview transcripts, and working notes
  - Date-stamp entries: `## YYYY-MM-DD — Meeting/Interview Topic`
  - Capture action items, decisions, and follow-ups
  - Tag attendees and note key quotes

Create files as needed — not all files are required from the start. Archive completed deliverables and past meeting notes to the archive/ folder.

### tasks.md — Kanban Format

Use a markdown kanban board with these columns:

```markdown
## Backlog

### Conduct finance team interviews
- **id**: mc-a1b2c3
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: discovery, interviews

### Benchmark against industry standards
- **id**: mc-p6q7r8
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: analysis, research

## In Progress

### Analyze current-state process maps
- **id**: mc-d4e5f6
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: analysis, process
- **started**: 2026-03-28

### Draft interim findings report
- **id**: mc-s9t0u1
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: deliverable, report
- **started**: 2026-03-26

## Client Review

### Technology assessment deck
- **id**: mc-g7h8i9
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: deliverable, technology
- **started**: 2026-03-22

## Delivered

### Stakeholder interview synthesis
- **id**: mc-j0k1l2
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: deliverable, discovery
- **completed**: 2026-03-20

### Project kickoff presentation
- **id**: mc-v2w3x4
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: deliverable, kickoff
- **completed**: 2026-03-12
```

Each card follows the Mission Control card format:
- **id** (required): Unique `mc-XXXXXX` identifier (auto-generated by `create_task_card`)
- **priority**: `low`, `medium`, `high`, or `critical`
- **tags**: Comma-separated labels (e.g. deliverable, discovery, analysis, interviews, report)
- **started** / **completed**: ISO date timestamps (auto-set by `move_task_card`)

Use `create_task_card` and `move_task_card` tools for all task management — tasks.md is a read-only rendering from the database and must not be edited directly.

### status.md — Engagement Status Format

```markdown
- **phase**: Discovery & Analysis — Week 4 of 8
- **health**: yellow
- **updated**: 2026-03-28
- **owner**: Engagement Lead

## Current Focus
- Current-state process mapping (operations and finance)
- Interim findings report draft — due April 2
- Technology assessment awaiting client feedback

## Blockers
- Finance VP unavailable until April 1 — delays 3 remaining interviews

## Budget
- Total hours: 320
- Used: 145 (45%)
- Remaining: 175
- Burn rate: on track

## Recent Milestones
- 2026-03-20: Stakeholder interview synthesis delivered — 12 interviews complete
- 2026-03-15: Current-state data collection finished
- 2026-03-12: Project kickoff with steering committee
```

Health values: `green` (on track, client engaged), `yellow` (deliverable at risk or awaiting client input), `red` (scope creep, missed deadlines, or relationship issues).

### timeline.md — Activity Log

Reverse-chronological event stream capturing engagement activity. New entries go at the top of the current day's section.

```markdown
## 2026-03-28

- 16:00 — Finding added: "Duplicate data entry across 3 systems" (category: process, severity: significant)
- 14:30 — Card mc-d4e5f6 moved to **In Progress** (was: Backlog) — "Analyze current-state process maps"
- 11:00 — Client meeting: Operations Director — reviewed process pain points
- 09:30 — Status health changed: green → yellow — Finance VP scheduling delay

## 2026-03-27

- 17:00 — Card mc-g7h8i9 moved to **Client Review** (was: In Progress) — "Technology assessment deck"
- 15:00 — Interview completed: IT Director — key insight on integration gaps
- 13:30 — New card created: mc-s9t0u1 "Draft interim findings report" (priority: high)
- 10:00 — Scope change request received — additional compliance review (+16 hours)
```

Events are auto-logged by `move_task_card` and status changes. Use `append_timeline_event` to manually log notable events (deliverables submitted, client meetings, findings recorded, scope changes, budget updates). Heartbeats can auto-append weekly engagement summaries for passive monitoring.

### plans.md — Structured Execution Plans (Read-Only Rendering)

Plans are stored in the MC database. `plans.md` is auto-generated after every state change — never edit it directly. Use `draft_plan` to create plans, and `update_plan_step`/`update_plan_status` for mutations. After approval, the plan executor automatically sequences step execution. Pull the planning skill for the full protocol.
