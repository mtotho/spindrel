## Workspace File Organization — Content / Editorial Calendar

Organize channel workspace files to manage content production and publishing:

- **calendar.md** — Editorial calendar and schedule
  - Content schedule organized by week and publish date
  - Format: `### Week of YYYY-MM-DD` with entries listing title, type, channel, deadline, status
  - Track key dates: draft due, review due, publish date
  - Flag upcoming deadlines within the next 7 days

- **ideas.md** — Content ideas backlog
  - Format: `### Idea Title` with topic, angle, target audience, priority, estimated effort
  - Group by content type (blog, social, video, newsletter, etc.)
  - Include source/inspiration and competitive notes
  - Move approved ideas to calendar.md with assigned dates

- **briefs.md** — Content briefs and outlines
  - Format: `### Content Title` with topic, target keywords, audience, key points, word count target
  - SEO notes: primary keyword, secondary keywords, search intent
  - Reference links and source material
  - Approval status and reviewer feedback

- **analytics.md** — Content performance metrics
  - Format: `### YYYY-MM — Monthly Performance` with top performers table
  - Track: views, engagement rate, conversions, shares
  - Content type breakdown and trend analysis
  - Quarterly comparison and insights

- **tasks.md** — Kanban board for content pipeline (Mission Control compatible)

- **status.md** — Content program health and current focus (Mission Control compatible)

- **timeline.md** — Reverse-chronological activity log (Mission Control compatible)
  - Auto-captures content events: pieces published, drafts submitted, reviews completed, performance milestones
  - Entries: `- HH:MM — Event description` grouped under `## YYYY-MM-DD` date headers
  - Append new entries at top of current day's section
  - Use `append_timeline_event` tool to log events (also auto-logged by `move_task_card`, status changes, etc.)

- **notes.md** — Editorial notes, style guides, and scratch space
  - Brand voice guidelines and tone references
  - Competitor content analysis
  - Meeting notes from editorial planning sessions
  - Seasonal content themes and campaign notes

Create files as needed — not all files are required from the start. Archive past monthly analytics and completed briefs to the archive/ folder.

### tasks.md — Kanban Format

Use a markdown kanban board with these columns:

```markdown
## Ideas

### Write beginner's guide to home composting
- **id**: mc-a1b2c3
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: blog, sustainability, evergreen

### Record Q2 product demo video
- **id**: mc-p6q7r8
- **priority**: low
- **tags**: video, product

## Drafting

### Newsletter: April industry roundup
- **id**: mc-d4e5f6
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: newsletter, monthly
- **started**: 2026-03-28

### Blog post: 5 trends reshaping remote work
- **id**: mc-s9t0u1
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: blog, thought-leadership
- **started**: 2026-03-27

## Editing

### Case study: Acme Corp migration success
- **id**: mc-g7h8i9
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: case-study, enterprise
- **started**: 2026-03-24

## Published

### Social campaign: Spring product launch
- **id**: mc-j0k1l2
- **priority**: high
- **tags**: social, campaign
- **completed**: 2026-03-22

### Blog post: Getting started with our API
- **id**: mc-v2w3x4
- **priority**: medium
- **tags**: blog, developer, tutorial
- **completed**: 2026-03-18
```

Each card follows the Mission Control card format:
- **id** (required): Unique `mc-XXXXXX` identifier (auto-generated by `create_task_card`)
- **priority**: `low`, `medium`, `high`, or `critical`
- **tags**: Comma-separated labels (e.g. blog, newsletter, social, video, campaign, evergreen)
- **started** / **completed**: ISO date timestamps (auto-set by `move_task_card`)

Use `create_task_card` and `move_task_card` tools for all task management — tasks.md is a read-only rendering from the database and must not be edited directly.

### status.md — Content Program Status Format

```markdown
- **phase**: Active Production — Q2 Content Calendar
- **health**: green
- **updated**: 2026-03-28
- **owner**: Content Lead

## Current Focus
- April newsletter draft due Friday
- Acme Corp case study in final review
- Spring campaign social assets publishing this week

## Blockers
- None currently

## Content Metrics (March)
- Published: 12 pieces (4 blog, 3 social, 3 newsletter, 2 video)
- Total views: 8,400
- Avg engagement rate: 4.2%
- Top performer: "API Getting Started Guide" — 2,100 views

## Recent Milestones
- 2026-03-22: Spring product launch campaign published across all channels
- 2026-03-18: Developer tutorial series kicked off
- 2026-03-10: Q1 content audit completed — 15 pieces refreshed
```

Health values: `green` (pipeline flowing, deadlines met), `yellow` (drafts overdue or pipeline bottleneck), `red` (missed publish dates or critical content gaps).

### timeline.md — Activity Log

Reverse-chronological event stream capturing content production activity. New entries go at the top of the current day's section.

```markdown
## 2026-03-28

- 16:00 — Published: "Spring Product Launch" social campaign — 3 platforms, 8 assets
- 14:30 — Card mc-g7h8i9 moved to **Editing** (was: Drafting) — "Case study: Acme Corp"
- 11:00 — Analytics update: March blog traffic up 18% MoM
- 09:00 — Card mc-d4e5f6 moved to **Drafting** (was: Ideas) — "Newsletter: April roundup"

## 2026-03-27

- 17:00 — Card mc-v2w3x4 moved to **Published** (was: Editing) — "Getting started with our API"
- 15:00 — Brief approved: Acme Corp case study — keywords finalized, interview scheduled
- 13:30 — New card created: mc-s9t0u1 "5 trends reshaping remote work" (priority: medium)
- 10:00 — Editorial planning meeting — Q2 themes finalized, 20 ideas prioritized
```

Events are auto-logged by `move_task_card` and status changes. Use `append_timeline_event` to manually log notable events (content published, briefs approved, performance milestones, editorial decisions). Heartbeats can auto-append weekly pipeline summaries for passive monitoring.

### plans.md — Structured Execution Plans (Read-Only Rendering)

Plans are stored in the MC database. `plans.md` is auto-generated after every state change — never edit it directly. Use `draft_plan` to create plans, and `update_plan_step`/`update_plan_status` for mutations. After approval, the plan executor automatically sequences step execution. Pull the planning skill for the full protocol.
