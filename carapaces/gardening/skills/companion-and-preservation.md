# Companion Planting & Preservation

## Companion Planting Reference

| Plant | Good Companions | Rationale |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| Tomatoes | Basil, marigolds, borage, parsley | Basil may deter aphids/thrips; marigolds deter nematodes; borage repels tomato hornworm |
| Peppers | Basil, carrots, marjoram | Basil may deter aphids |
| Squash | Nasturtiums, borage, beans | Nasturtiums trap aphids; borage deters hornworms; beans fix nitrogen |
| Cucumbers | Dill (young), nasturtiums, sunflowers | Sunflowers provide trellis + attract predatory wasps |
| Beans | Almost everything | Fix atmospheric nitrogen — good neighbor crop |
| Brassicas | Nasturtiums, dill, chamomile | Nasturtiums trap aphids; dill attracts parasitic wasps |
| Herbs (most) | Interplant freely | Aromatic herbs generally confuse pest insects |

**Note:** Companion planting evidence is mixed in controlled studies. These are best-practice combinations with anecdotal and some empirical support — not magic. Spacing, soil, and water matter more.

## Preservation Quick Reference

| Produce | Best Methods | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Tomatoes | Water bath can, freeze, dehydrate | Acidic enough for water bath without pressure canner |
| Peppers | Freeze (no blanching needed), ferment, pickle | Freeze in strips — easy for winter cooking |
| Beans | Pressure can, freeze (blanch 3 min first) | Water bath not safe for beans |
| Cucumbers | Pickle (water bath), refrigerator pickles | |
| Herbs | Dry, freeze in oil, freeze in water | Basil: freeze only — drying loses flavor |
| Garlic | Cure 3-4 weeks in warm dry place, braid | |
| Squash (winter) | Cure at 80°F 10 days, store cool and dry | 50-55°F ideal, months of storage |
