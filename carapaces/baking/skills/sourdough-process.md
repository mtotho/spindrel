# Sourdough Process

## Sourdough Starter

### Building a Starter from Scratch (~14 days)

| Day | Action | Expected |
|-----|--------|----------|
| 1 | 50g whole wheat flour + 50g water (75°F), loosely cover | Nothing yet |
| 2-3 | No feeding, just observe | Possible slight activity |
| 4 | Discard to 25g, add 25g WW flour + 25g water | Bubbles forming |
| 5-7 | Daily discard + feed 1:1:1 (starter:flour:water) | Rises, smells funky/acetone |
| 8-14 | Switch to AP or bread flour 1:1:1 | Doubles in 4-8h, tangy smell |

Whole wheat or rye accelerates early fermentation due to native yeasts and bran nutrients. Once active, transition to white flour for more predictable behavior.

### Maintenance

| Scenario | Method |
|----------|--------|
| Daily baking | Counter, 1:1:1 every 24h |
| Occasional baking | Fridge, 1:5:5 weekly |
| Long neglect (>2 weeks) | Discard all but 10g, feed daily 5-7 days to rebuild |
| Travel / freeze | Feed, let peak, freeze; thaw and revive with daily feeds |

**Peak timing** is when starter has doubled and just starts to dome or slightly recede — this is the ideal moment to use it. Float test is unreliable; use timing and visual dome.

### Starter Health Diagnostics

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| No rise after 12h | Weak culture, too cold, or chlorinated water | Use filtered/rested water; move to 75-80°F; feed daily |
| Rises but smells like nail polish remover | Acetic acid dominant (too cold, too much discard) | Warmer temps (78°F+), more frequent feeds |
| Smells like sulfur/rotten | Leuconostoc bacteria (early stage) | Normal at day 2-4; keep feeding through it |
| Hooch (grey liquid on top) | Hungry — yeast exhaled alcohol | Pour off, feed; increase feed ratio (1:2:2 or 1:3:3) |
| Pink or orange streaks | Serratia marcescens contamination | **Discard everything. Sanitize jar. Start fresh.** |
| Mold (fuzzy, any color) | Surface contamination | **Discard. Start fresh.** |

## Baker's Math

### Hydration Formula

```
Hydration % = (Total Water / Total Flour) × 100

Example: 350g water / 500g flour = 70% hydration
```

### Scaling Any Recipe

Fix your flour weight first, then multiply everything by that weight using baker's %.

```
Target flour: 500g
Water at 70%:  500 × 0.70 = 350g
Starter at 20%: 500 × 0.20 = 100g
Salt at 2%:     500 × 0.02 = 10g
```

### Hydration Effects on Dough

| Hydration | Crumb | Handling | Good For |
|-----------|-------|----------|---------|
| 60-65% | Tight, sandwich-style | Easy to shape | Beginner loaves, sandwich bread |
| 70-75% | Open, chewy | Moderate | Standard sourdough boule |
| 78-85% | Very open, holes | Sticky, requires technique | Bâtard, ciabatta-style |
| 85%+ | Extremely open | Needs strong flour + experience | Advanced bakers only |

**Flour matters for hydration:** Whole wheat and bread flour absorb more water than AP. If substituting 10-20% whole wheat, add 2-5% more water.

## Desired Dough Temperature (DDT)

DDT is the most important number in timing your fermentation correctly. Target 75-78°F for standard sourdough.

```
Water Temp = (DDT × 3) − Flour Temp − Room Temp − Friction Factor

Friction Factor: ~5°F for hand mixing, ~15°F for stand mixer
```

**Example (winter, kitchen 68°F, flour 65°F, DDT target 76°F):**
```
Water Temp = (76 × 3) − 65 − 68 − 5 = 228 − 138 = 90°F
```

Use a probe thermometer. Even 5°F off can shift bulk ferment by 1-2 hours.

## Standard Sourdough (70% Hydration)

| Ingredient | Baker's % | Example (500g flour) |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Bread flour | 100% | 500g |
| Water | 70% | 350g |
| Active starter | 20% | 100g |
| Salt | 2% | 10g |

### Full Timeline

```
[Feed Starter] → 4-8h to peak
      ↓
[Autolyse] 30-60 min (flour + ~90% of water, no starter/salt)
      ↓
[Mix] Add starter, mix 3-5 min. Add salt + reserved water. Incorporate fully.
      ↓
[Bulk Ferment] 4-10h depending on temp (see below)
  - First 2h: Coil folds or stretch & folds every 30 min (3-4 sets)
  - Remaining time: Hands off until ready
      ↓
[Pre-shape] Gentle round, bench rest 20-30 min
      ↓
[Final Shape] Boule or bâtard, into floured banneton seam-up
      ↓
[Cold Proof] 8-16h in fridge at 38-40°F
      ↓
[Bake] Preheat Dutch oven at 500°F for 45-60 min
  - Score, bake covered 20 min (steam phase)
  - Remove lid, reduce to 450°F, bake 20-25 min (crust phase)
      ↓
[Cool] 1-2h on wire rack before cutting (internal must reach 210°F)
```

### Bulk Ferment Endpoint Detection

**This is the hardest part of sourdough.** Do not rely on time alone.

**Signs bulk is done (need 2-3 of these):**
- Dough has increased **50-75% in volume** (not doubled — that's usually overproofed)
- **Dough feels airy and jiggly**, not tight and springy
- **Bubbles visible on sides and bottom** of container
- **Passes the poke test**: wet finger poke springs back slowly (5-10s). Fast spring = underproofed. No spring / stays dented = overproofed.
- Dough **releases cleanly** from container walls
- Surface looks **domed and slightly puffy**, not flat

**Time as a guide (not a rule):**
| Room Temp | Bulk Time |
|-----------|-----------|
| 65°F (winter) | 8-12h |
| 70°F (fall/spring) | 6-8h |
| 75°F (target) | 4-6h |
| 80°F+ (summer) | 3-5h |

**When in doubt, slightly underproof > overproof.** Underproofed dough has oven spring to recover. Overproofed dough cannot be fixed.

### Folding Methods

| Method | When to Use | How |
|--------|------------|-----|
| Stretch & fold | 65-75% hydration | Grab one side, stretch up and fold over. Rotate 90°, repeat 4x = 1 set |
| Coil fold | 75%+ hydration | Slide hands under dough center, lift, let ends fold under. Rotate 90°, repeat |
| Lamination | Very high hydration, add-ins | Wet surface, spread dough flat, fold like letter, stretch again |

## Dutch Oven Baking

The Dutch oven creates the steam environment critical for oven spring and crust development.

**Setup:**
- Preheat Dutch oven inside oven at 500°F for **minimum 45 min** — the mass needs to saturate with heat
- Use cast iron (Lodge, Stargazer) or enameled cast iron (Le Creuset, Staub)
- Score cold dough straight from fridge — cold dough holds shape better

**Scoring:**
- Use a lame (razor blade) or sharp serrated knife at **30-45° angle** to the surface, not perpendicular
- Main score: single slash down the long axis, offset from center, 1/2 inch deep
- The angle creates the "ear" — the flap that opens and blisters
- Secondary decorative scores should be shallower (1/4 inch)
- **Score decisively** — hesitation creates drag and tearing

**Bake phases:**
| Phase | Temp | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|------|---------|
| Covered | 500°F | 20 min | Steam traps moisture, allows oven spring |
| Uncovered | 450°F | 20-25 min | Maillard reaction, crust color and crunch |

**Internal temp target: 205-210°F**. Use a probe thermometer.

## Crumb Troubleshooting

| Crumb Appearance | Likely Cause | Fix |
|-----------------|-------------|-----|
| Gummy / dense throughout | Underbaked or cut too soon | Bake to 210°F internal; rest 2h before cutting |
| Dense at bottom, open at top | Underproofed | Extend bulk or cold proof |
| Flat, no holes anywhere | Weak starter, overproofed, or both | Use starter at true peak; reduce bulk time |
| Huge irregular holes + gummy patches | Overproofed | Shorten bulk; use colder proofing temp |
| Tight crumb but good spring | Low hydration or heavy flour | Increase hydration 3-5%, consider bread flour |
| Thick rubbery crust, no ear | Insufficient steam or scored wrong | Longer preheat; score at angle |
| Pale crust, no color | Too short uncovered bake | Extend uncovered phase; higher rack position |
| Burnt bottom | Dutch oven too close to element | Place on middle rack; put sheet pan underneath |

## Flour Guide

| Flour | Protein % | Absorption | Best For |
|-------|-----------|------------|---------|
| AP (all-purpose) | 10-12% | Moderate | Beginner sourdough, lower hydration |
| Bread flour | 12-14% | High | Higher hydration, better gluten network |
| Whole wheat | 13-14% | Very high | 10-20% sub for flavor/nutrition |
| Rye | 13% (weak gluten) | Very high | Starter boost, adds sourness |
| Spelt | 12-13% (fragile gluten) | High | Mix max 20% — overworks easily |
| 00 | 10-12% | Low | Pizza, focaccia, not sourdough |

**Safe substitution rule:** Never exceed 30% whole grain in your first few loaves. Whole grain weakens gluten structure and accelerates fermentation.

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Winter (Dec-Feb) — Kitchen ~65-68°F
- Use 90-95°F water to hit DDT target of 76°F
- Bulk ferment will be 8-12h — start in morning for evening shape
- Consider proofing inside oven with just the light on (adds ~5-8°F)
- Longer cold proof is fine — flavor actually improves up to 18h

### Spring/Fall (Mar-May, Sep-Nov) — Kitchen ~68-72°F
- Standard timeline applies: 4-6h bulk at 75°F
- Monitor dough more closely — swings between warm and cool days

### Summer (Jun-Aug) — Kitchen ~78-82°F
- Use 60-65°F water
- Bulk can finish in 3-4h — **check at 3h and every 30 min after**
- Consider doing bulk in a cooler corner or near AC
- Reduce starter to 15% to slow fermentation
- Add salt immediately after mixing (delays fermentation slightly)
- Cold proof overnight is non-negotiable in summer
