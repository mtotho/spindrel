# Fermentation Reference

## Kombucha

**F1 (Primary, 7-10 days):** 1 cup starter + 1 SCOBY per 8 cups sweet tea (1 cup sugar per gallon). Cover with cloth. Done when pH 2.5-3.5 or tart to taste.

**F2 (Carbonation, 2-4 days):** Bottle with flavor add-ins, seal tight. Burp daily to check carbonation. Refrigerate when bubbly.

| Flavor | Per 16oz Bottle |
|--------|----------------|
| Ginger Lemon | 1" fresh ginger + juice of 1/2 lemon |
| Berry Mint | 1/4 cup muddled berries + 5 mint leaves |
| Apple Cinnamon | 1/4 apple (thin sliced) + 1 cinnamon stick |
| Hibiscus Lime | 1 tbsp dried hibiscus + 1/2 lime juice |

## Lacto-Fermentation (Vegetables)

**Brine:** 2% salt by weight (20g per 1000g water)

| Vegetable | Salt % | Temp | Days | Done When |
|-----------|--------|------|------|-----------|
| Cabbage (kraut) | 2% dry | 65-72°F | 5-10 | Sour, tangy, crunchy |
| Carrots | 2% brine | 65-72°F | 4-6 | Sour, still firm |
| Cucumbers | 2% brine | 68-72°F | 3-5 | Sour, no longer raw |
| Peppers | 2% brine | 68-72°F | 7-14 | Funky, sour |
| Garlic | 2% brine | 65°F | 14-21 | Mellow, fermented smell |

**Key rules:** Keep vegetables submerged (use a weight), loose lid or airlock, room temp. Move to fridge when desired sourness is reached — cold halts fermentation.

## Quick Reference Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Autolyse | Flour + water rest (no starter/salt) to hydrate flour and begin gluten formation |
| Bulk ferment | First fermentation after mixing, before shaping |
| Cold proof | Refrigerated final proof to slow yeast and develop flavor |
| DDT | Desired Dough Temperature — the target temp for mixed dough |
| Ear | The raised, blistered crust flap that forms where the dough was scored |
| Extensibility | Dough's ability to stretch without snapping |
| Hooch | Grey liquid (alcohol) that forms on top of an unfed starter |
| Lame | Razor blade tool used for scoring |
| Oven spring | Rapid rise in oven from CO2 expansion and steam |
| Scoring | Pre-bake cuts that direct oven spring and form the ear |
| Windowpane test | Stretch a thin piece of dough — if it stretches thin and translucent without tearing, gluten is well-developed |
