# Spindrel Product Context

## Register

product

## Purpose

Spindrel is an operational workspace for people who run work through bots, channels, sessions, widgets, projects, memory, and review loops. The product should help users understand what is happening, decide what needs attention, and move into the right work surface without extra ceremony.

## Users

- Operators who repeatedly scan the workspace for unread replies, active sessions, scheduled work, health issues, and Mission Control findings.
- Builders who use Spindrel to develop and review projects, inspect evidence, and hand work between human and agent workflows.
- Administrators who configure bots, channels, integrations, providers, widgets, schedules, and system settings.

## Product Tone

Spindrel should feel calm, dense, and trustworthy. It is not a marketing site, a novelty dashboard, or a toy control panel. The best surfaces feel like a capable desktop app: low chrome, quick to scan, direct about state, and clear about the next useful action.

## Product Principles

- Make operational state visible through real data, not explanatory filler.
- Preserve familiarity where users expect it: sidebars, headers, rows, tabs, filters, forms, and drilldowns should behave like established product UI.
- Use visual hierarchy to guide the eye. Low chrome still needs focal points.
- Prefer durable review surfaces over transient drawers or modal detours.
- Keep mobile and desktop structurally related, with layout adapting to the viewport rather than becoming a different product.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS card grids with repeated icon, metric, label, and supporting text.
- Decorative gradients, glass panels, glowing borders, and visual effects that do not carry workflow state.
- Washed-out pages where every section has equal weight and the eye has no clear starting point.
- Heavy admin chrome with repeated dividers, boxed bars, and filled buttons for routine actions.
